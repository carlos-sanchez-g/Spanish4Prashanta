Strategy &
Learning Method

How this plan is built, what level you are targeting, and why this approach works for your situation.

The four tracks

You will learn on four tracks at once

Track 1
Survival Spanish First
From day one you learn phrases you can actually use: greetings, asking for help, introducing yourself. This prevents the common mistake of studying grammar for weeks without being able to say a word.
Track 2
Pronunciation Before Grammar
Spanish pronunciation is regular and logical — once you know the rules, you can read anything. Master the five vowels, word stress, and key sounds (r, rr, ñ, j, ll) in the first two weeks.
Track 3
Grammar as a Tool, Not Theory
You study grammar only when it helps you communicate. No abstract rules — only patterns you can use immediately. Each week introduces one grammar structure in context.
Track 4
Speaking From Week 1
Apps alone are not enough. You must speak every day — out loud, to yourself, to a language exchange partner. Record yourself on your phone. Listening + speaking = real learning.

Rules for
Success

These are not suggestions — they are the habits that separate people who learn Spanish from people who almost learn Spanish.

1
Speak before you feel ready
You will never feel ready. Everyone feels foolish in the first weeks. Start speaking badly and improve. Silence is the enemy of language learning.
2
Learn full phrases, not isolated words
Never learn "hotel = hotel." Learn: "El hotel está cerca." "La habitación está lista." "Voy al hotel." Context makes vocabulary stick permanently.
3
Repeat key phrases until they are automatic
Professional Spanish requires instant phrases. You should not need to think when someone says ¿Qué necesita? — the answer should come automatically.
4
Stop translating — from Week 5
Translation is useful at the very beginning. From Week 5, train your brain to go directly from situation to Spanish: "Quiero agua. Necesito ayuda. El cliente está aquí." No English step in between.
5
Record yourself every day
Uncomfortable? Yes. Powerful? Extremely. Listen back, correct one or two mistakes, re-record. Keep your recordings. In three months you will be amazed at the difference.
6
Change your phone to Spanish
From Week 3, switch your phone language to Spanish. You will see 100+ Spanish words every day with zero extra effort. Uncomfortable the first week — highly effective by the third.
7
Protect your study time like work
If your professional future depends on Spanish, Spanish must be treated like a job, not a hobby. Block 90 minutes in your calendar. Do not cancel it for small reasons.
8
Embrace confusion — it means you are learning
When something confuses you, that discomfort is your brain working. Do not stop and look up everything. Keep going — context often clarifies. Only look up what blocks comprehension.
9
Connect with real Spanish speakers
italki and HelloTalk are not optional from Week 3. One real human conversation will give you more motivation than a week of solo study. People are the real reward of language learning.
10
Plateaus are not failure — push through
Around Weeks 3–4 and again Weeks 7–8, many learners feel they have stopped improving. They have not — their brain is consolidating. The breakthrough comes right after the plateau. Keep going.

Month 1 — Week 1
Sounds, Alphabet & Survival Phrases
Goal: Feel comfortable with Spanish sounds and basic phrases. By the end of this week, Prashanta, you should be able to introduce yourself in 5 sentences, say 10 greetings, and read any Spanish word correctly.
Pre-A1
Days 1–7
🎯 Priority this week: Master Spanish pronunciation before anything else. Spanish has 5 short, clear vowels (a, e, i, o, u) — very different from English. Once you know pronunciation rules, you can read any Spanish word correctly. Do not rush past this week — correct pronunciation now saves months of bad habits later.
What to learn
  • The Spanish alphabet — 27 letters

  • Subject pronouns: yo, tú, usted, él, ella, nosotros

Month 1 — Week 3
Present Tense & Daily Routine
Goal: Describe your daily routine in 8+ sentences. By end of Week 3, you can conjugate regular verbs, use negatives, and talk about what you do every day.
A1
Days 15–21
🎯 Priority this week: Spanish verbs change their ending for each person — this is called conjugation. There are 3 verb types: -AR (hablar), -ER (comer), -IR (vivir). Each follows regular rules. Learn these patterns and you instantly know how to use hundreds of verbs. Start Coffee Break Spanish this week.
What to learn
  • -AR verbs: hablar, trabajar, escuchar, estudiar, comprar, caminar, visitar
  • -ER verbs: comer, beber, leer, ver, entender
  • -IR verbs: vivir, escribir, abrir
  • Days of the week and time
  • Negation: No hablo español / No entiendo
  • Time adverbs: siempre, nunca, todos los días, por la mañana
Resources to use this week
  • Language Transfer: Lessons 13–20
  • Coffee Break Spanish: Start Season 1, Episodes 1–2
  • Duolingo/Busuu: daily 15 min — verbs & routines
  • Dreaming Spanish: 20 min/day Superbeginner
  • Anki: Add all new verbs with full conjugation example
  • italki: Find your first language exchange partner this week

Month 2 — Week 6

Goal: Explain your work in Spanish, use formal usted, and write a professional self-description. This is directly relevant to your professional future.

  • Telephone phrases: ¿Podría hablar con...? Un momento, por favor
  • Email basics: Estimado/a, Le saluda, Atentamente
